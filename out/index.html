What is an Inventory Audit — and Why It Matters

An inventory audit is the process of verifying a company's actual inventory against its financial records. It's essential for maintaining accuracy in stock levels, avoiding shrinkage, ensuring compliance with accounting standards, and preparing for financial statements or investor reports.

Types of Inventory Audits We Offer

Cycle Counting

Periodic verification of a portion of inventory to ensure ongoing accuracy.

Full Physical Inventory

Comprehensive stock check done annually or quarterly.

Spot Checks

Surprise audits on specific SKUs or product categories.

Third-Party Verification

Independent validation for compliance or investor due diligence.

Digital Audit Integration

Audit support for ERP, WMS, or POS systems like SAP, Oracle, Zoho, etc.

Common Inventory Issues We Solve

Inconsistent stock records across warehouses
Shrinkage due to theft or misplacement
Overstocking or understocking
Discrepancies between purchase orders and actual stock
ERP syncing issues and ghost inventory

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a full audit or just a cycle count?

If you're running regular operations, a cycle count may be sufficient. We can help assess what's best.

Can you work with my accounting team or ERP system?

Yes! We integrate with most accounting systems and collaborate with internal teams.

How long does an inventory audit take?

It depends on your stock size, location(s), and system complexity — usually from 1 to 7 days.

Is the audit remote or on-site?

We offer both depending on your needs and data availability.
